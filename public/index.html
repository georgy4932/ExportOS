Capabilities

Everything from contract to sealed evidence pack

ExportOS is a system of record — not a payments or trading platform. It helps teams organise the documents and statuses used for export compliance review.

📄

Contracts

Record export contracts with commodity, quantity, value, buyer, and Incoterms. Status tracks from active through completed.

🚢

Shipments

Log individual shipments under a contract. Track NXP reference, vessel details, and proceeds reconciliation status.

📋

Bills of Lading

Upload original B/L references. Repatriation deadlines are calculated from shipment documentation and tracked against each export record.

💳

Payment Evidence

Record SWIFT receipts and credit advice. Discrepancy status (CLEAN / DISCREPANT) is computed against your tolerance settings.

⚖

Reconciliation

Allocate payment receipts to shipments. Partial and full proceeds status is tracked automatically as receipts are allocated.

✅

Compliance Checklist

Per-shipment checklist: NXP approved, CCI obtained, B/L uploaded, payment evidence uploaded, credit advice confirmed.

🔒

Bank Evidence Packs

Generate and seal evidence packs for submission to your commercial bank. Sealing is blocked until the full checklist is complete.

📁

Audit Trail

Every data change is recorded with timestamp and user. Audit events are immutable — they cannot be edited or deleted.

Who it’s for

Built for the people who manage non-oil exports

ExportOS is designed for the teams responsible for documenting and evidencing compliance — not trading desks or payment processors.

Export Operators

Record contracts and shipments as they happen. Keep repatriation deadlines visible before they become a problem.

Compliance Officers

Maintain the compliance checklist for each shipment. Confirm NXP approval, CCI, and credit advice in one place.

Freight & Logistics Teams

Upload Bill of Lading references and track shipment status from vessel booking through final allocation.

Trade Finance Teams

Generate sealed bank evidence packs for submission. The system enforces completeness before sealing is allowed.

Why ExportOS

Replace spreadsheets and email threads with a structured record

Most Nigerian non-oil exporters manage compliance across a mix of spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups, and email attachments. That works until it doesn’t.

Without ExportOS

  • ✗Repatriation deadlines tracked in a shared spreadsheet that isn’t always current
  • ✗SWIFT receipts arrive by email; someone manually matches them to the right shipment
  • ✗Compliance checklist is a PDF form that gets emailed back and forth
  • ✗Evidence packs assembled manually from folders of scanned documents
  • ✗No audit trail — who updated the NXP status and when?

With ExportOS

  • ✓Repatriation deadlines are tracked against each shipment and kept visible in the dashboard
  • ✓Each receipt is allocated to a shipment; discrepancy status computed automatically
  • ✓Compliance checklist is live on each shipment record, updated in the dashboard
  • ✓Evidence packs sealed only when the full checklist is complete — enforced in the database
  • ✓Immutable audit events on every change — timestamp and user recorded automatically
